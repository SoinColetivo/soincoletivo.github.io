Brazilian multimedia collective Soín Coletivo announces new drifting and rythm game lunr.rdio.taxi
São Paulo, Brazil, May 18, 2025 - Brazilian queer collective Soín Coletivo is proud to announce it’s debut title: lunr.rdio.taxi – an innovative mix between drifting and rhythm games, creating a genre-blend dubbed Drifthm. A playable demo is expected to hit steam in the following months and release is planned for Q1 2026.

Watch the latest teaser trailer on Youtube.
In lunr.rdio.taxi, you will slide through psychedelic spacetime tunnels, drifting between obstacles placed in sync with the soundtrack and scoring points, while piloting the stylish ships from a vast cast of characters – each with their own unique story. Playing through each track feels like dancing with the ship, in a blend of gameplay, colors and sounds that will put you into the groove of a curated selection from the best of Brazil’s electronic music.
Soín Coletivo is developing the game in collaboration with Speedtest – An underground brazilian electronic music label – to bring tracks from some of the hottest DJs currently playing in brazilian clubs, raves and dance parties. The game presents a side of brazilian urban culture and electronic dance music that is rarely featured in video games, which makes it unique and deeply attuned to the overall culture of our times.
Listen to more of Speedtest’s music on Bandcamp.
The full release – scheduled for 2026 – will feature our own solar system’s nine planets (yes, it’s counting Pluto), each featuring around four levels/tracks. The playable demo that Soín expects to release later this year will feature the first planet, Mercury, with its four levels, and a bonus planet.

About Soín Coletivo:
Soín Coletivo is a creative collective from Brazil almost entirely made up of queer and neurodivergent folk, working on a multitude of projects, with its debut title being lunr.rdio.taxi. Developers and artists from the collective have previously worked on Relic Hunters: Rebels (Rogue Snail, Netflix), Black Finger JET (KOHACHI STUDIO) and Atomic Picnic (BitCake Studio, Mad Mushroom), among others.
